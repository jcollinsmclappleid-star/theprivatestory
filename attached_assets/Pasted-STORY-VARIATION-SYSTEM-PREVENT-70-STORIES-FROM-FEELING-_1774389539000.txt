STORY VARIATION SYSTEM — PREVENT 70 STORIES FROM FEELING THE SAME

IMPORTANT:
Keep the 10–12 minute structure and sensual quality rules, BUT do NOT generate every story with the same emotional rhythm, opening style, pacing, setting type, dialogue density, or ending mood.

The 6-part structure remains:
1. Hook
2. Setup
3. Build-up / tension rise
4. Main romantic / sensual scene
5. Emotional payoff / connection
6. Soft ending / last line

However, every story must also generate a unique STORY DNA before writing.

-----------------------------------
ANTI-REPETITION RULES
-----------------------------------

1. Do not reuse the same exact opening device in consecutive stories.
Rotate hooks between:
- held gaze
- unexpected touch
- a door closing
- hearing their voice first
- seeing them after time apart
- being alone together unexpectedly
- being interrupted then resumed
- a confession nearly spoken
- a tense invitation
- a dangerous private moment

2. Do not reuse the same setting more than 2 times in a batch of 20 stories.

3. Do not reuse the same emotional_engine + setting_type + relationship_dynamic combination.

4. Do not use the same signature phrases repeatedly.
Avoid repeating lines like:
- “the room seemed to narrow”
- “a breath she didn’t realise she was holding”
- “heat between them”
- “lingering gaze”
Any phrase used in one story should be varied or avoided in the next 5 stories.

5. Do not make every story peak the same way.
Vary the main sensual scene:
- dialogue-heavy intensity
- silence and body language
- reunion closeness
- roleplay power tension
- emotional confession before intimacy
- interrupted moment resumed later
- public restraint / private release

6. Do not make every ending soft and tender.
Rotate endings so some feel:
- warm
- unresolved
- dangerous
- addictive
- emotionally healing
- quietly possessive

-----------------------------------
BATCH GENERATION RULES FOR 70 STORIES
-----------------------------------

When generating a batch of stories, maintain a story registry.

For each story, store:
- title
- category
- subtheme
- setting_type
- relationship_dynamic
- emotional_engine
- tension_style
- narrative_perspective
- pacing_style
- ending_mood
- visual_motif
- signature_object

Before writing a new story, compare against the registry and avoid repeating the same overall combination.

Target diversity across every 10 stories:
- at least 5 different settings
- at least 4 different emotional engines
- at least 3 different narrative perspectives / voice combinations
- at least 4 different ending moods
- at least 4 different relationship dynamics

-----------------------------------
WRITING RULE
-----------------------------------

Use the sensual story structure as the base, but let the STORY DNA determine:
- how the hook feels
- how quickly tension builds
- what kind of chemistry dominates
- what sensory details are emphasized
- how the emotional payoff lands
- what kind of ending lingers

The result should feel like the same premium brand, but not the same story template repeated.

-----------------------------------
BRAND CONSISTENCY RULE
-----------------------------------

All stories should still feel:
- premium
- cinematic
- sensual
- emotionally immersive
- tasteful
- adult
- replayable

Do NOT make them feel identical.
They should feel like different films inside the same universe.

-----------------------------------
SIMPLE IMPLEMENTATION RULE
-----------------------------------

For every story:
1. Generate STORY DNA first
2. Check against prior story registry
3. Adjust if too similar
4. Then write the 10–12 minute story using that DNA
