# Editorial Standard — Public Sample Content

**Owner:** Ianson System Ltd t/a The Private Story
**Scope:** All audio and text content served on public, unauthenticated routes — including the central `/samples` "Editor's Picks" landing page.
**Last reviewed:** 2026-05

## Purpose

The Private Story sells personalised, fully-explicit, AI-narrated adult fiction behind a paid, age-verified, signed-in subscription. The public surface of the site exists to communicate the brand, the writing quality, the voice quality, and the emotional register — **not** to deliver pornography.

This editorial standard defines the test every piece of public sample content must pass before it ships. It exists primarily to demonstrate, both internally and to any regulator, that we have applied a deliberate, written, and consistent rule to what may appear publicly.

## The test

A public sample piece may be published if and only if **all** of the following are true.

1. **No depiction of a sex act in progress.** The narrative may build to the threshold of physical intimacy and stop there. Once a piece crosses into a sex act being performed, it is no longer eligible for public publication and must be moved behind authentication, age verification, and payment.
2. **No anatomical reference to genitalia or to penetration**, in any voice, by either the narrator or a character.
3. **No description of orgasm, ejaculation, or masturbation**, by either the narrator or a character.
4. **No description of nudity beyond shoulder, collarbone, hand, foot, or hair.** Removing one item of jewellery or one item of outer clothing (a cardigan, a coat) is acceptable. Removing further clothing is not.
5. **The closing image is anticipatory, not active.** Acceptable closing images include: a door not yet closed, a hand not yet taken, a step not yet taken, a question not yet answered, a key turning in a lock, a held breath. Unacceptable closing images include: any kissing past a single moment of contact, any touch beyond a hand on a shoulder or collarbone, any clothing being removed.
6. **All consent is explicit, on-page, and given by an adult.** Every character whose desire is depicted is unambiguously an adult, and every escalation is preceded by an explicit ask and an explicit yes (or an explicit, unambiguous "no" that is honoured).
7. **Cover imagery follows the same rule.** No nudity, no faces in close-up implying sexual activity, no skin reveal beyond shoulder or collarbone. Atmospheric over portraiture.

## Cliffhanger framing

Every public sample is built as a "cliffhanger" — a complete short narrative that lands at the door, the question, the threshold. The brand promise is that the *reader's own* story, when she creates one, can continue past the threshold. That promise (and the gating behind it) is communicated on the page itself in three places: the AgeGate copy, a per-card "Ends on:" tag that previews the closing image, and the final-section CTA.

## Why this standard

The standard is conservative by design. It is intended to:

- Sit comfortably below the threshold for an "adult-only service" (defined, in the OSA s.79(2) sense, as a service whose principal purpose is the encountering of pornographic content) when judged solely on the public surface of the site.
- Reduce the risk that a public-facing sample is itself classifiable as pornographic content under s.79(2).
- Make a clear, defensible distinction between *the brand surface* (publicly accessible, narratively suggestive, never explicit) and *the product* (privately accessible, fully explicit, behind authentication + age verification + payment).

This is a *content* standard. It does not replace the platform-level age verification, payment gating, signed-URL audio delivery, transcript privacy, or the moderation pipeline that protect the explicit product itself. Those controls are documented separately in the platform safety report.

## Reviewer checklist (one-pager)

Before any new public sample is shipped:

- [ ] Read the script aloud end-to-end. Does the closing image meet the anticipatory test (rule 5)?
- [ ] Search the script for any anatomy or sex-act vocabulary (rule 2, 3).
- [ ] Confirm consent is explicit and on-page (rule 6).
- [ ] Confirm all parties are unambiguously adult, with no schoolyard or family-relation framing (rule 6).
- [ ] Inspect the cover image. Does it meet rule 7?
- [ ] Confirm the per-card "Ends on:" tag accurately previews the closing image.
- [ ] If the script falls outside the standard, route it to the *paid, signed-in, age-verified* product instead. Do not edit it down to fit the public surface.

## Versioning

Material changes to this standard must be reviewed by the founder and recorded in this file's git history. The version of the standard in force at the time a piece was published is the version it must be measured against.
