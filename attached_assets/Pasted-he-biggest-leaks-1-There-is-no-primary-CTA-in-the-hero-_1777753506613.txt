he biggest leaks
1. There is no primary CTA in the hero. The hero has a badge, h1, and tagline — and that's it. To do anything, the visitor has to scroll past the hero into the Three Doors block. On mobile that's a full thumb-swipe before they see a single button. Adding a single "Create your [trope] story — under 3 minutes" button directly in the hero, with the trope pre-selected when they land in /create, would likely move the needle more than anything else.

2. The Three Doors block sits between the hero and the page content. A visitor who searched "billionaire romance audio" and landed on the billionaire page is then asked "Romance, After Dark, or Drift?" — a generic decision they didn't come to make. This dilutes the trope-specific intent. On trope/audience landing pages, the Three Doors should drop below the page body (or be replaced by a single Create button that pre-selects the trope and sends them straight into the brief).

3. No audio sample anywhere on the landing page. Adult audio is a "I need to hear it before I trust it" purchase. Right now the only way to hear what Mistral Large + the narration voices actually sound like is to sign up, age-verify, pay, and write a brief. A 30–60 second sample player in the hero — ideally one per top voice — would cut the trust gap dramatically. This is what Quinn, Dipsea, and Bloom all do.

4. Price is invisible until they leave the page. Visitors have to click to /pricing to find out it's ~£24.99/month or ~£19.92/month annual. Premium pricing isn't the problem — hiding it is. Putting "From £19.92/mo • 5 stories included • cancel anytime" near the hero CTA filters out price-sensitive bouncers earlier and reassures the rest.

5. No social proof. The trust block is all feature claims ("completely private", "premium narration"). There's no "X women listening", no testimonials, no review quotes, no press mention. The "2.6M+ combinations" number is a maths fact, not social proof. Even one or two short anonymous listener quotes per page (different ones per trope) would help the female-first / privacy-led story land.

Mid-impact
6. Generic CTA copy. "Create my story" appears everywhere. On the billionaire page it should be "Create your billionaire romance" — same on every trope page. This reinforces that the page they're on is the page they should buy from.

7. No first-story incentive. A "Your first story is £1" or "Try one for £1, no subscription" tier would convert curious clickers who won't commit to a £24.99/mo subscription on first visit. This is the standard playbook for premium adult subscription products. (You already have a Stripe one-off addon at £3.99 for active subscribers — a non-subscriber single-purchase variant could be added.)

8. Bundle weight. The Vite build warning showed a 2.73 MB JS bundle (730 KB gzipped). On mobile in the UK on a marginal connection, that's a 2–4 second delay before anything is interactive. Some of that is unavoidable (audio player, motion library), but a manualChunks split for the SEO landing route would help — these pages don't need the create-flow, gift-flow, or audio-player code in the initial load.

9. The hero h1 is long and literary. "Billionaire Romance Audio Stories — The Man Whose Attention Is the Resource" is beautiful prose, but on mobile it wraps to ~5 lines and pushes the rest of the page below the fold. Consider keeping the literary hook as a sub-headline and using a tighter h1.

Lower-impact but worth doing
10. FAQ schema. Adding <script type="application/ld+json"> with the FAQ schema would get the FAQs into Google's expandable rich result. Doesn't change conversion directly, but improves CTR from search — and you have the FAQ data already structured.

11. Sticky bottom-of-screen CTA on mobile. Once a visitor scrolls past the hero, the create button disappears. A small persistent "Create your billionaire romance →" bar at the bottom of the viewport on mobile keeps the conversion path one tap away through the whole page.

12. Exit-intent offer on desktop / scroll-to-bottom offer on mobile. Capture the bouncer with "Get one free story for signing up" or similar.

My order of operations if I were doing this
If you want me to actually build these: I'd do 1, 2, 4, 6 in one pass — they're all small edits to SEOPage.tsx and the configs, and together they should be the biggest move. Then 3 (sample player) as the next focused piece, since it needs an audio asset decision and a small new component. Then 5, 7, 11 as a third pass. 8 is a separate optimisation pass.
